// research interests
Bridging learning and physical intelligence
My research spans the full pipeline from algorithmic foundations to real-world deployment on robotic platforms.

Reinforcement Learning

Developing sample-efficient RL algorithms for continuous control, with focus on policy optimization and reward shaping for robotic tasks.

Sim-to-Real Transfer

Bridging the reality gap through domain randomization, system identification, and adaptive transfer techniques for robust real-world deployment.

Robot Manipulation

Teaching robots dexterous manipulation skills — grasping, assembly, and tool use — using learning-based approaches combined with tactile sensing.

Imitation Learning

Learning from demonstrations using behavioral cloning, inverse RL, and human-in-the-loop feedback to accelerate robot skill acquisition.

Foundation Models for Robotics

Leveraging large pre-trained models (vision-language, diffusion) as priors for generalizable robotic perception, planning, and control.

Multi-Agent Systems

Coordination and collaboration among multiple robots through decentralized learning, communication protocols, and emergent cooperative behavior.
