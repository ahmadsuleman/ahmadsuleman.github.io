About

Ahmad Suleman is an AI researcher working in the areas of robot learning, reinforcement learning, and autonomous intelligent systems. He received his BSc Electronic Engineering from UCET IUB in 2010-14. He received MPhil Microelectronic Engineering from Punjab University, Lahore in 2016-18. Since last 3 years , he has been serving as an Assistant Manager at the Artificial Intelligence Technology Center (AITeC), National Centre for Physics, Islamabad, Pakistan, where he works in the Autonomous AI and Decision Support Lab. He has organized and delivered four annual national-level training workshops for professionals from the high-technology industry.

He is also collaborating remotely as a Research Assistant with Sultan Qaboos University, Oman, conducting research on digital twin modeling and perception-aware autonomous vehicle interaction in 5G-V2X environments.

Prior to joining AITeC, he spent two years working as an independent Reinforcement Learning Research Engineer, delivering research and development projects for international clients through freelance platforms and professional networks.

He has authored and co-authored research published in leading venues including IEEE Transactions on Affective Computing, IEEE Transactions on Vehicular Technology, IEEE Wireless Communications Magazine, IEEE Sensors Journal, IEEE Internet of Things Journal, and IEEE Access. He is currently serving as reviewer of several prestigious Journals like IEEE Transactions on Vehicular Technology, IEEE Access , IEEE Transactions on Network and Service Management, Springer Nature Soft Computing, and Springer Nature Energy Systems

His research focuses on reinforcement learning for autonomous control, multimodal perception, and simulation-to-real transfer for robotics and intelligent transportation systems. His work integrates deep learning, robotic simulation, and real-world robotic deployment to develop adaptive and reliable autonomous systems.

Research Interests
His research explores learning-based algorithms and their deployment in real-world autonomous and robotic systems.

Reinforcement Learning for Autonomous Systems

Developing reinforcement learning methods for sequential decision-making, policy optimization, and adaptive control in complex environments, with applications in intelligent agents, autonomous vehicles, and cyber-physical systems.

Sim-to-Real Transfer

Bridging the gap between simulation and real-world deployment using domain randomization, system identification, and adaptive transfer techniques to enable robust policies on physical robotic platforms.

Autonomous Vehicles & Intelligent Transportation

Designing AI-driven perception and decision-making systems for connected and autonomous vehicles, including V2X-enabled intelligence, driver behavior modeling, and digital twin–based traffic analysis.

Multi-Modal Sensor Fusion for Robust Perception

Developing learning-based sensor fusion methods that integrate heterogeneous modalities such as vision, LiDAR, acoustic, and contextual signals to enhance perception reliability in degraded and uncertain environments, including low-visibility, noisy, and sensor-impaired conditions.

Embodied AI & Robot Learning

Building embodied intelligent agents that learn through interaction with the physical world, integrating perception, reasoning, and control. Exploring vision–language–action (VLA) foundation models to enable generalizable task understanding and adaptive behavior in embodied systems.

Digital Twins & Connected Systems

Building data-driven digital twin models of cyber-physical systems to monitor, predict, and optimize large-scale intelligent infrastructures such as transportation networks and connected environments.
